## SCRIPT

### SEGMENT 1 — COLD OPEN (~8 seconds, ~19 words)
**Purpose:** Pattern interrupt. Establish the paradox immediately.
**Visual:** Split screen — Mbappé celebrating World Cup goal LEFT, Mbappé head down at club RIGHT. Hard cut.

> "Kylian Mbappé. World Cup hero. Club football flop. Same player. Two completely different realities."

**On-screen text:** "WORLD CUP HERO." → hard cut → "CLUB FLOP." (bold white on black, animated — text slides in from left, holds, then hard cuts to next)

### SEGMENT 2 — HOOK (~52 seconds, ~117 words)
**Purpose:** Establish the pattern. Tease the breakdown.
**Visual:** B-roll of Mbappé World Cup goals (2018, 2022), then B-roll of Mbappé at PSG/Madrid looking frustrated. Fast cutting.

> "In 2018, at nineteen, he won the World Cup. In 2022, he scored a hat-trick in the final — the second player ever to do so, after Geoff Hurst in 1966. For France, he is untouchable. A generational talent who delivers on the biggest stage. But at club level? PSG spent seven years building their entire project around him. They never won the Champions League. The season he left, they did. Real Madrid signed him for free, and the trophies stopped coming. Same player, same result, every time. In this video, we are breaking down exactly why Mbappé performs at World Cups but flops at clubs — and whether he can carry France to another World Cup title."

**On-screen text:** "WHY?" (animated — scales up from 0 to full size, holds for 2 seconds, fades out)

---

### SEGMENT 3 — THESIS (~14 seconds, ~33 words)
**Purpose:** Explicit thesis statement.
**Visual:** Close-up of Mbappé, slow-mo. Then hard cut to tactical board graphic.

> "The answer is not about ability. Mbappé is elite everywhere he plays. It is about context — what the World Cup gives him that club football simply cannot."

**On-screen text:** "IT'S NOT ABILITY." → "IT'S CONTEXT." (animated — first phrase types out letter by letter, then dissolves into second phrase)

### SEGMENT 4 — ACT 1: WHAT THE WORLD CUP GIVES HIM (~106 seconds, ~247 words)
**Purpose:** Establish the World Cup context — the system that lets Mbappé thrive.
**Visual:** France national team footage — Mbappé with Griezmann, Giroud, Kanté. Team chemistry, tactical structure. Didier Deschamps on the sideline.

> "With France, Mbappé does not have to be the system. He is a weapon inside one. Didier Deschamps has been France manager since 2012 — over a decade of continuity, of building a structure that does not depend on any single player. In that system, Olivier Giroud is the target man. He occupies centre-backs, he holds the ball, he does the unglamorous work that nobody tweets about. Antoine Griezmann is the creator — dropping deep, finding pockets, linking midfield to attack. And Mbappé? His job is simple. Stay high. Stay left. Run behind. Finish. That is it. Every other responsibility is handled by players who understand their role and accept it. At a World Cup, you do not have time for ego. You have seven games. You either fit into the system or you go home. France chose to build a system that fits Mbappé — not the other way around. Deschamps never asked Mbappé to press like a madman. He never asked him to track back. He never asked him to change his game. He built the team around Mbappé's strengths and covered his weaknesses with structure. That is the World Cup advantage. Seven games. Fixed system. Clear roles. No time for anyone to reshape the team around their preferences."

**On-screen text:** "WEAPON IN A SYSTEM" (animated — fades in over B-roll, lower-third style)

### SEGMENT 5 — ACT 2: WHY CLUBS BREAK AROUND HIM (~160 seconds, ~373 words)
**Purpose:** The club failure pattern — why the same player fails in a different context.
**Visual:** PSG footage (Mbappé with Neymar, Messi), Madrid footage (Mbappé with Vinícius, Bellingham). Tactical overlaps, left-wing collision.

> "At club level, the dynamic flips completely. Clubs sign Mbappé for record money, and suddenly the system has to revolve around him — not because he demands it explicitly, but because the investment demands it. The board expects it. The fans expect it. The sponsors expect it. At PSG, that meant a complex power struggle with Neymar and Messi — not simple accommodation, but competing egos and tactical disagreements that ultimately contributed to the team's inconsistency. Neymar wanted the ball. Messi wanted the ball. Mbappé wanted the ball. Three players who all needed to be the focal point, sharing one pitch. At Real Madrid, it was different but equally destructive. Vinícius Junior had spent three years turning the left wing into the most dangerous corridor in world football. When Mbappé arrived, they formed a fluid partnership on the left — but two world-class left wingers sharing the same space is not a partnership. It is a collision. And the player who paid the biggest price was not either of them. It was Jude Bellingham. In his debut season, Bellingham scored twenty-three league goals playing as an advanced attacking midfielder. When Mbappé arrived, Bellingham's role evolved. He dropped deeper. He covered wider. He became a utility piece, patching structural holes that Mbappé's presence created. A player who was supposed to be the future of the club became a janitor, cleaning up messes he did not make. The numbers tell the story. Both PSG and Madrid invested heavily in Mbappé. Both PSG and Madrid got worse. Not because Mbappé is bad — he is extraordinary — but because the system bent around the star instead of the star fitting into the system. And once a system bends, it breaks. At a World Cup, there is no time for this. Seven games. The system is fixed. Nobody has time to bend."

**On-screen text:** "SYSTEM AROUND A WEAPON" (animated — slides in from right, red background, contrast with Act 1's blue)

### SEGMENT 6 — ACT 3: THE TACTICAL COST (~134 seconds, ~313 words)
**Purpose:** Measurable evidence of the cost — defensive numbers, pressing stats, win percentage.
**Visual:** Tactical graphics, pressing heat maps, stat overlays. B-roll of Mbappé not pressing, teammates compensating.

> "The numbers tell the rest of the story, and they are brutal. When the striker at the top of the press does not press, the line behind him does not hold. It is simple physics — a defensive structure is a chain, and if the first link is missing, the whole chain fails. Pressing efficiency drops. Defensive recoveries drop. The team starts defending from a position of constant emergency, chasing the game instead of controlling it. At Real Madrid, when Mbappé was absent, the win percentage climbed to around eighty-three percent — compared to roughly seventy-one percent with him in the squad. The shape came back. Vinícius found his territory again. The team remembered it had a function as a unit, not just a stage for a star. When Mbappé returned, the problems returned with him. Same as they always had at every club before this one. Mbappé recorded nine tackles across the entire campaign. Nine. Rodrigo — a player with half his profile and a fraction of his contract — recorded nineteen. When the man at the top of the press does not press, the midfield has to cover more ground. When the midfield covers more ground, they arrive late to attacks. When they arrive late to attacks, the forwards get frustrated. When the forwards get frustrated, the dressing room fractures. It is a cascade — one missing piece collapses the entire structure. At a World Cup, this does not happen. Not because Mbappé suddenly starts pressing — he does not. But because France's system is built to account for it. Giroud presses. Griezmann presses. Kanté presses. The system absorbs Mbappé's defensive absence. At clubs, nobody absorbs it. They just suffer it."

**On-screen text:** "9 TACKLES. ALL SEASON." (animated — number counts up from 0 to 9, then stops, holds for impact)

### SEGMENT 7 — ACT 4: THE PATTERN ACROSS CLUBS AND COUNTRY (~166 seconds, ~387 words)
**Purpose:** Show the pattern is consistent — and that France is the exception that proves the rule.
**Visual:** Montage — PSG managers (Tuchel, Pochettino, Galtier, Enrique), Madrid managers (Ancelotti, Alonso), then France (Deschamps). Graphic showing the cycle.

> "The pattern is unmistakable. At PSG, Thomas Tuchel built a genuinely structured side. He took them to a Champions League final. He was forced by the club to apologise to Mbappé after attempting to hold him accountable for disciplinary reasons — Mbappé and another player were late to a team meeting because they wanted to watch a match on television. Tuchel left. Within eighteen months, he won the Champions League with Chelsea. Mauricio Pochettino replaced him and stopped coaching almost immediately. He started managing personalities instead of systems. The football drifted from any coherent identity. Christophe Galtier lasted barely a year, because the role he offered Mbappé did not match promises made elsewhere. Luis Enrique came in, refused to bend, and won everything — the season Mbappé left. At Real Madrid, Carlo Ancelotti's famous dressing room harmony — the glue that held those Champions League nights together — never fully recovered once Mbappé arrived. Xabi Alonso, a coach who built his entire identity around defensive discipline and collective structure, was fired mid-season. The results were not catastrophic. The football was not broken beyond repair. But the locker room had fractured, and the fracture ran directly through one relationship — Alonso and Mbappé. When the board sided with the player over the coach, the message was clear. The manager is not the authority here. The star is untouchable. And once that message lands, no tactical setup in the world fixes it. But with France? Deschamps has been there since 2012. Thirteen years. He has never been forced out. He has never had to apologise. He has never lost the dressing room. Why? Because with France, Mbappé accepts the system. He does not have a choice. The World Cup is every four years. You do not get to reshape a national team around your preferences. You fit in, or you watch from home. At clubs, you sign a five-year contract and the board will fire the manager before they fire you. That is the difference."

**On-screen text:** "SAME PLAYER. SAME PATTERN. EVERY CLUB." (animated — each word appears one at a time, building to full sentence)

### SEGMENT 8 — ACT 5: WHY FRANCE DOESN'T HAVE THIS PROBLEM (~79 seconds, ~184 words)
**Purpose:** The key insight — national team context vs club context.
**Visual:** France team huddle, Mbappé celebrating with teammates, Deschamps giving tactical instructions.

> "The reason France gets the best version of Mbappé is simple. They do not ask him to be the system. They ask him to be a weapon inside one. The system is bigger than any player. The badge is bigger than the brand. At clubs, that equation flips. The investment, the wage, the marketing — it all demands the team revolve around the star. And once that message lands, once the manager is no longer the authority and the star becomes untouchable, no tactical setup in the world fixes it. France never let that happen. Deschamps never let that happen. The World Cup does not let that happen. Seven games. Fixed system. Clear roles. Mbappé fits in because he has to. And when he fits in, he is the most dangerous player on the planet."

**On-screen text:** "FRANCE: WEAPON IN A SYSTEM" → "CLUBS: SYSTEM AROUND A WEAPON" (animated — split screen, blue left side / red right side, text animates in simultaneously)

### SEGMENT 9 — ACT 6: THE VERDICT — CAN HE CARRY THIS WORLD CUP? (~92 seconds, ~215 words)
**Purpose:** Forward-looking prediction.
**Visual:** Mbappé in France kit, looking determined. World Cup trophy. Upcoming World Cup imagery.

> "So can Mbappé carry France to another World Cup? The answer is: he does not have to carry them. That is the entire point. France does not need Mbappé to carry them. They need him to finish. To stay high. To run behind. To do the one thing he does better than anyone alive. The question is not whether Mbappé will show up at the World Cup. He always shows up at World Cups. The question is whether France can keep giving him the context that lets him thrive — the fixed system, the clear roles, the team that does not ask him to be something he is not. If they can, he will score goals. If they cannot — if the national team starts to bend around him the way every club has — then the same pattern that broke PSG and Madrid will break France too. But here is the thing. France has resisted that pattern for thirteen years. Deschamps has resisted it. The World Cup format resists it. Seven games is not enough time for a player to reshape a national team. And that is exactly why Mbappé performs at World Cups. Not because he is different. Because the context is different."

**On-screen text:** "HE DOESN'T HAVE TO CARRY THEM." (animated — text fades in slowly, holds for 3 seconds)

**Purpose:** Closing. The prediction.
**Visual:** Split screen again — Mbappé World Cup celebration LEFT, Mbappé club frustration RIGHT. Slow push in. Then hard cut to black.

> "Mbappé is not broken. He is the same player everywhere — elite, lethal, decisive. What changes is the context. World Cups give him a system he cannot break. Clubs give him a system that breaks around him. The data does not lie. At World Cups, he is a champion. At clubs, he is a symptom. The question for the next World Cup is not about Mbappé. It is about whether France can resist the urge to do what every club has done — and let the system stay bigger than the star."

**On-screen text:** "THE SYSTEM IS BIGGER THAN THE STAR." (animated — text types out one word at a time, each word appears with a subtle zoom pulse, final word "STAR" holds for 3 seconds, then fades to black)

## ANIMATION NOTES (for Editor)

All on-screen text must be ANIMATED, not static. Animation styles:
- **Slide in:** text enters from left/right edge
- **Scale up:** text grows from 0 to full size
- **Type out:** text appears letter by letter (typewriter effect)
- **Dissolve:** text crossfades between phrases
- **Hard cut:** instant text replacement (for impact)
- **Zoom pulse:** subtle scale animation on individual words

These can be achieved with:
- ffmpeg's `drawtext` filter with `enable` and `x`/`y` expressions for slide/scale
- Remotion for complex animations (if available)
- Pre-rendered animated PNGs/GIFs with Pillow + frame sequencing
