## SCRIPT

### SEGMENT 1 — COLD OPEN (~8 seconds)
**Purpose:** Pattern interrupt. Establish the paradox immediately.
**Visual:** Split screen — Mbappé celebrating World Cup goal LEFT, Mbappé head down at club RIGHT. Hard cut.

**Voiceover:**
> "Kylian Mbappé has won a World Cup, scored a hat-trick in a World Cup final, and is arguably the deadliest finisher of his generation. He's also never won a Champions League. Same player. Two completely different realities."

**On-screen text:** "WORLD CUP HERO. CLUB FLOP." (bold white on black, hard cut between the two phrases)

### SEGMENT 2 — HOOK (~52 seconds)
**Purpose:** Establish the pattern. Tease the breakdown.
**Visual:** B-roll of Mbappé World Cup goals (2018, 2022), then B-roll of Mbappé at PSG/Madrid looking frustrated. Fast cutting.

**Voiceover:**
> "In 2018, at nineteen, he won the World Cup. In 2022, he scored a hat-trick in the final — the first player to do that since 1966. For France, he's untouchable. But at club level? PSG spent seven years building around him. They never won the Champions League. The season he left, they won it without him. Real Madrid signed him. Same result. Trophies stopped coming."
>
> "In this video, we're breaking down exactly why Mbappé performs at World Cups but flops at clubs — and whether he can carry France to another World Cup title."

**On-screen text:** "WHY?" (appears at the end, bold white on dark background)

**Purpose:** Explicit thesis statement.
**Visual:** Close-up of Mbappé, slow-mo. Then hard cut to tactical board graphic.

**Voiceover:**
> "The answer isn't about ability. Mbappé is elite everywhere. It's about context — what the World Cup gives him that club football can't."

### SEGMENT 4 — ACT 1: WHAT THE WORLD CUP GIVES HIM (~106 seconds)
**Purpose:** Establish the World Cup context.
**Visual:** France national team footage — Mbappé with Griezmann, Giroud, Kanté, Pogba. Team chemistry, tactical structure. Didier Deschamps on the sideline.

**Voiceover:**
> "With France, Mbappé doesn't have to be the system. He's a weapon inside one. Deschamps built a team that doesn't need Mbappé to press, to hold the ball, to pin defenders. They have Giroud for that. They have Griezmann to create. They have a midfield that controls tempo without asking Mbappé to drop deep."
>
> "Mbappé's job is simple: stay high, stay left, run behind, finish. That's it. Every other responsibility is handled by players who understand their role and accept it."
>
> "At a World Cup, you don't have time for ego. You have seven games. You either fit into the system or you go home. France chose to build a system that fits Mbappé — not the other way around."

### SEGMENT 5 — ACT 2: WHY CLUBS BREAK AROUND HIM (~160 seconds)
**Purpose:** The club failure pattern.
**Visual:** PSG footage (Mbappé with Neymar, Messi), Madrid footage (Mbappé with Vinícius, Bellingham). Tactical overlaps, left-wing collision.

**Voiceover:**
> "At club level, the dynamic flips. Clubs sign Mbappé for record money, and suddenly the system has to revolve around him — not because he demands it, but because the investment demands it. The board, the fans, the sponsors all expect the team to be built around the star."
>
> "At PSG, that meant Neymar and Messi had to accommodate him. At Madrid, it meant Vinícius Jr. — who had spent three years making the left wing the most dangerous corridor in world football — had to share the space."
>
> "The result was the same at both clubs: two left-side attackers chasing the same five yards of grass. No partnership. No link play. Just two players running parallel careers on the same team."
>
> "And the player who paid the biggest price wasn't either of them. It was the midfielder — Bellingham at Madrid, Verratti at PSG — who had to drop deeper, cover wider, fill the structural holes that Mbappé's presence created."

### SEGMENT 6 — ACT 3: THE TACTICAL COST (~134 seconds)
**Purpose:** Measurable evidence of the cost.
**Visual:** Tactical graphics, pressing heat maps, stat overlays. B-roll of Mbappé not pressing, teammates compensating.

**Voiceover:**
> "The numbers tell the story. When the striker at the top of the press doesn't press, the line behind him doesn't hold. Pressing efficiency drops. Defensive recoveries drop. The team starts defending from a position of constant emergency."
>
> "At Madrid, when Mbappé was absent, the win percentage climbed. The shape came back. Vinícius found his territory again. The team remembered it had a function as a unit. When Mbappé returned, the problems returned with him."
>
> "This isn't a criticism of Mbappé's ability. He's one of the most lethal finishers the game has produced. But elite finishing and elite teamwork aren't the same thing. And when the system has to bend around one player, the structure breaks."
>
> "At a World Cup, there's no time for this to happen. Seven games. The system is fixed. The roles are clear. Nobody has time to bend."

### SEGMENT 7 — ACT 4: THE PATTERN ACROSS CLUBS AND COUNTRY (~166 seconds)
**Purpose:** Show the pattern is consistent — and that France is the exception.
**Visual:** Montage — PSG managers (Tuchel, Pochettino, Galtier, Enrique), Madrid managers (Ancelotti, Alonso), then France (Deschamps). Graphic showing the cycle.

**Voiceover:**
> "The pattern is unmistakable. At PSG: Tuchel built a structured side, took them to a Champions League final, was forced to apologize to Mbappé for holding him accountable. He left. Pochettino stopped coaching, started managing personalities. Galtier lasted a year. Enrique won everything the season Mbappé left."
>
> "At Madrid: Ancelotti's dressing room harmony never recovered. Alonso was fired mid-season because the locker room fractured — and the fracture ran through Mbappé."
>
> "But with France? Deschamps has been there since 2012. Thirteen years. He's never been forced out. He's never had to apologize. Why? Because with France, Mbappé accepts the system. He doesn't have a choice. The World Cup is every four years. You don't get to reshape a national team around your preferences — you fit in, or you watch from home."

### SEGMENT 8 — ACT 5: WHY FRANCE DOESN'T HAVE THIS PROBLEM (~79 seconds)
**Purpose:** The key insight — national team context vs club context.
**Visual:** France team huddle, Mbappé celebrating with teammates, Deschamps giving tactical instructions.

**Voiceover:**
> "The reason France gets the best version of Mbappé is simple: they don't ask him to be the system. They ask him to be a weapon inside one. The system is bigger than any player. The badge is bigger than the brand."
>
> "At clubs, that equation flips. The investment, the wage, the marketing — it all demands the team revolve around the star. And once that message lands, no tactical setup in the world fixes it."

**On-screen text:** "FRANCE: WEAPON IN A SYSTEM. CLUBS: SYSTEM AROUND A WEAPON."

### SEGMENT 9 — ACT 6: THE VERDICT — CAN HE CARRY THIS WORLD CUP? (~92 seconds)
**Purpose:** Forward-looking prediction.
**Visual:** Mbappé in France kit, looking determined. World Cup trophy. Upcoming World Cup imagery.

**Voiceover:**
> "So can Mbappé carry France to another World Cup? The answer is: he doesn't have to carry them. That's the point. France doesn't need Mbappé to carry them. They need him to finish. To stay high. To run behind. To do the one thing he does better than anyone alive."
>
> "The question isn't whether Mbappé will show up at the World Cup. He always shows up at World Cups. The question is whether France can keep giving him the context that lets him thrive — the fixed system, the clear roles, the team that doesn't ask him to be something he's not."
>
> "If they can, he'll score goals. If they can't — if the national team starts to bend around him the way clubs do — then the same pattern that broke PSG and Madrid will break France too."
